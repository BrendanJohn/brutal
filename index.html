Brutalist architecture in the Boston area

What is Brutalism?

Brutalism is a unique architectural style that flourished worldwide in the 1960s and early 1970s. The word itself comes from a French word "beton brut", which translates to raw concrete. Brutalism is characterized by hulking slabs of unpainted concrete. The structures are imposing and bulky, fortress-like in nature with large walls and few windows.
